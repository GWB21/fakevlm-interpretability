SECTION 2: Attention Masking Experiment (Language Bias Analysis)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Hypothesis: If the model exhibits language bias, masking the top-20%
visually attended patches will not change the prediction output,
indicating the model's response is driven by language priors rather
than visual evidence.

  Interpretation:
    Language Bias Rate exceeds 60%. The model maintains its prediction
    in the majority of cases after critical visual patches are removed,
    strongly supporting the language bias hypothesis. The LLM prior
    dominates over visual evidence in FakeVLM's decision process.

SECTION 3: Integrated Conclusion
----------------------------------------------------------------------
  1. Frequency Domain: Non-trivial performance changes under frequency
     filtering indicate partial reliance on specific frequency bands.
     Further investigation with varying radius values is recommended.

  2. Language Bias: High bias rate (95.68%) confirms that the LLM
     component of FakeVLM exerts strong influence on final predictions,
     potentially overriding visual evidence. This represents a systemic
     vulnerability: adversarial text prompts may manipulate detection
     outcomes regardless of image content.
